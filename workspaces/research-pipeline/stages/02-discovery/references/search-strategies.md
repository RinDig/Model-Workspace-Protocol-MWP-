# Search Strategies

Patterns for effective information gathering.

---

## Query Construction

**Start broad, narrow down:**
- Begin with 2-3 key terms
- Add filters (date, type, domain) if too many results
- Use OR to include synonyms
- Use NOT to exclude irrelevant domains

**Example progression:**
1. `AI safety research`
2. `AI safety research 2023-2024 peer-reviewed`
3. `("AI safety" OR "AI alignment") research 2023-2024 -news`

---

## Source Diversity

Aim for multiple source types:
- Academic papers (Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar)
- Industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey, company blogs)
- Primary sources (official docs, APIs, specifications)
- Community knowledge (Stack Overflow, Reddit, forums)
- News and analysis (TechCrunch, Ars Technica)

---

## Credibility Signals

**High credibility:**
- Peer-reviewed publication
- Official documentation
- Multiple citations
- Recent updates
- Clear methodology

**Medium credibility:**
- Industry reports
- Established news sources
- Expert blog posts
- Conference talks

**Low credibility (use cautiously):**
- Opinion pieces
- Social media posts
- Unattributed content
- Outdated sources

---

## Coverage Mapping

After initial search, check:
- [ ] Every key question has sources
- [ ] Multiple perspectives on contentious topics
- [ ] Both supporting and contradicting evidence
- [ ] Recent and historical context
