# Research Methodology Guide

Select the right approach for your research question.

---

## Methodologies

| Methodology | Best For | Delivers |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Literature Review** | Understanding existing knowledge | Synthesis of published work |
| **Case Study** | Deep dive into specific instances | Detailed analysis of 1-3 cases |
| **Comparative Analysis** | Finding similarities/differences | Side-by-side evaluation |
| **Trend Analysis** | Understanding change over time | Pattern identification |
| **Gap Analysis** | Finding missing knowledge | Map of what is unknown |
| **Framework Application** | Testing theories in practice | Practical implementation insights |

---


## Scope Definition

**Depth vs Breadth Trade-off:**

| Approach | Sources | Questions | Coverage |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| Deep | 10-20 per question | 2-3 questions | Narrow, thorough |
| Balanced | 5-10 per question | 4-5 questions | Moderate |
| Broad | 3-5 per question | 6-7 questions | Wide, shallower |

**Scope Boundaries to Define:**

1. **Time range:** How recent must sources be?
2. **Geographic scope:** Any regional limitations?
3. **Source types:** Academic, industry, news, primary?
4. **Topic boundaries:** What is explicitly out of scope?
5. **Depth indicator:** Comprehensive overview vs focused analysis
